While browsing online I cam across several examples of Pi artworks especially published by The Guardian and Visual Cinnamon> The idea was to create something which was not very informative but more of an art.

The Start

I started off with working on illustrator assigning every number a color, plotting the digit and its immediate successor. Also a small circle in the bottom to show if the digit under review and its immediate next digit is the same.

The Change

It was almost impossible to plot all its digits on illustrator and so I started learning R which is a programming language that supports statistical computing and graphics. Since I had to spend no time cleaning the data, as it already was, I established a basic algorithm to create steps in a 2D field where the directions and color became defined by the digit itself. â€¨Starting from 3 at point (0,0) on the graph, every digit was at 36 degree angle from its previous digit. For the orders above 1000 digits and more, the above approach had become a colorful explosion. As I increased the digits gradually, I became more interested in seeing the walk they took.
